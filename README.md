# Marketing Budget Optimization Using Linear Regression

## Project Title
**Data-Driven Ad Channel Budget Allocation and Revenue Optimization via Multiple Linear Regression**

---

## Business Problem
Modern businesses allocate capital across a variety of marketing channels, including TV, radio, social media, search engine ads, and influencers. Without clear statistical visibility into channel performance, companies often run into major marketing inefficiencies—over-funding channels with diminishing returns while under-investing in high-performance avenues.

This project implements a **Multiple Linear Regression model** to predict sales revenue based on spending across five distinct ad channels. By calculating the exact return contribution of each channel, this analysis removes the guesswork from budget management, helping the business scale down underperforming assets and reallocate funds to maximize overall returns.

This is a **regression problem** because our target variable (`Sales_Revenue`) is continuous and numeric, making it ideal for linear relationship evaluation rather than discrete label classification.

---

## Dataset Description

### Data Structural Profile
The asset comprises historical advertising campaign registers, tracking regional allocations and financial inputs across five marketing vectors.

### Feature Matrix Definitions
* **CampaignID / Month / Region:** Unique marketing identifier index strings tracking timeline tracking details and regional market placements.
* **TV_Spend ($X_1$):** Total financial investment allocated toward television network commercials.
* **Radio_Spend ($X_2$):** Total financial investment allocated toward audio radio broadcasts.
* **SocialMedia_Spend ($X_3$):** Total financial investment allocated toward organic/paid interactive social feeds.
* **SearchAds_Spend ($X_4$):** Total financial investment allocated toward search engine optimization placements.
* **Influencer_Spend ($X_5$):** Total financial investment allocated toward creator endorsements.
* **Sales_Revenue ($Y$ - Target):** The continuous numeric sales revenue generated by the campaign, serving as our dependent target variable.

---

## Data Cleaning Summary
To secure clear, bias-free regression weights, we completed the following data cleaning workflow:

1.  **Missing Value Imputation:** We addressed 4 missing records in the `SocialMedia_Spend` column by filling them with the channel's median value, protecting the dataset from row loss.
2.  **Deduplication Validation:** We ran a complete duplicate check, confirming that no double-logged campaigns were distorting our data.
3.  **Data Type Synchronization:** Continuous financial entries were converted to standard floating-point numbers to guarantee smooth numerical processing.

---

## EDA Insights
* **Sales Distribution Profile:** The `Sales_Revenue` target variable shows a clean, normal distribution bell curve, making it a great fit for linear model assumptions.
* **Core Correlation Strengths:** The correlation matrix shows that **Radio Spend** and **Search Ads Spend** have the strongest direct relationships with sales, highlighting them as key drivers of customer response.
* **Linear Trajectories:** Visual scatter plots confirm a steady, straight-line relationship between advertising investments and revenue increases, showing no complex non-linear distortions.

---

## Regression Model Summary
We trained a **Multiple Linear Regression model** to map out the connections between spending channels and revenue. The model generated a baseline intercept of **`161.1005`** and calculated the following individual feature weights:

* **TV_Spend:** `+1.4451`
* **Radio_Spend:** `+3.4145`
* **SocialMedia_Spend:** `+0.6962`
* **SearchAds_Spend:** `+2.6744`
* **Influencer_Spend:** `+0.2024`

---

## Model Evaluation Results
Our performance metrics on the validation dataset highlight a highly reliable model:

* **Mean Absolute Error (MAE):** `19.8672` (On average, our model's revenue predictions are off by less than $20).
* **Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE):** `30.2155` (Confirms that our model is resilient against extreme, unexpected revenue spikes).
* **R² Score (Coefficient of Determination):** **`0.8591`** (This means our five marketing spend features successfully explain **85.91%** of the total variance in sales revenue).

---

## Coefficient Interpretation and Channel Efficiency

Our regression weights translate directly into clear business performance metrics:

1.  **Radio Spend (Highly Efficient):** Generates a coefficient of **`3.4145`**. This means every single dollar invested in radio ads returns **$3.41** in incremental sales revenue, making it our most efficient channel.
2.  **Search Ads Spend (Highly Efficient):** Generates a coefficient of **`2.6744`**. This means every single dollar invested returns **$2.67** in incremental sales revenue, showing exceptional performance.
3.  **TV Spend (Moderately Efficient):** Returns **$1.45** per dollar invested. It covers its cost and drives stable returns, making it a reliable choice for broad brand awareness.
4.  **Social Media Spend (Low Efficiency):** Returns **$0.70** per dollar invested. Because this return falls below our $1.00 breakeven point, this channel currently operates at a net financial loss.
5.  **Influencer Spend (Ineffective):** Generates a minimal coefficient of **`0.2024`**. Returning just **$0.20** per dollar spent, this channel is highly inefficient under its current strategy.

---

## Final Budget Recommendations

Based directly on our regression weights and efficiency analysis, we recommend the following strategic budget changes:

### 1. Scale Up Funding for High-Performance Channels
* **Actionable Recommendation:** *Radio Spend and SearchAds_Spend generate exceptional returns ($3.41 and $2.67 per dollar spent, respectively). The company should reallocate unspent capital to these high-performing channels to directly maximize revenue growth.*

### 2. Reduce Budgets for Low-Efficiency Options
* **Actionable Recommendation:** *SocialMedia_Spend and Influencer_Spend return values well below our $1.00 breakeven mark ($0.70 and $0.20, respectively). The company must reduce budgets for these underperforming channels by 40% and audit their creative strategies before allocating any future capital.*

### 3. Maintain Balanced TV Allocations
* **Actionable Recommendation:** *TV_Spend delivers a solid, dependable return of $1.45 for every dollar spent. The business should maintain its current TV budget to secure baseline brand visibility while using high-performing channels to drive immediate sales.*

### 4. Strategic Risks and Missing Data Constraints
* **Actionable Recommendation:** *Before making major budget adjustments, management should consider market saturation risks—doubling the radio budget may not automatically double returns due to audience fatigue. To improve future models, the company should start tracking competitor ad spend and seasonal promotional calendars.*
